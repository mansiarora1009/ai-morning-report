# Morning Report — 2026-04-28

## Top 3 Things Worth Paying Attention To

### 1. GitHub Copilot goes usage-based June 1 — and agentic runs cost 1000x more tokens
**What it is:** GitHub announced Copilot moves to usage-based billing on June 1. Same week: a new study on SWE-bench Verified found agentic coding consumes ~1,000x more tokens than chat/code reasoning, with up to 30x token variance across identical task runs. Copilot's own pricing doc confirms the multipliers — GPT-5.4 fast = 2x, GPT-5.5 fast = 2.5x.

**Why it matters:** The flat-rate era for coding AI is ending. For anyone using agentic workflows — Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code — the economics are about to become visible in ways they weren't before. The 1000x stat is the real story: most people don't understand how much more expensive "let the AI run" is than "ask the AI a question." This is the conversation that's going to dominate developer Twitter in June.

**Format:** Shift

**Reel angle:** "GitHub Copilot just switched to pay-per-use — and agentic AI uses 1000x more tokens than chat. Here's what your bill is about to look like."

### 2. OpenAI–Microsoft reset: the AGI clause is gone and OpenAI is now everywhere
**What it is:** OpenAI updated its Microsoft partnership — Microsoft remains the primary cloud, but OpenAI can now sell its models across all clouds. AWS CEO Andy Jassy confirmed OpenAI models are coming to Bedrock in weeks. Simon Willison noted the new language effectively kills the old AGI clause (which would have returned technology to OpenAI once AGI was reached). Product and model commitments extend to 2032; revenue share through 2030.

**Why it matters:** Two things changed at once: (1) OpenAI is no longer Azure-only, which reshapes how enterprises and developers access its models. (2) The AGI clause — the legal trip-wire that would have restructured everything if AGI arrived — is quietly gone as OpenAI becomes a PBC. The distribution story matters practically; the AGI clause story matters philosophically. Both are worth knowing.

**Format:** Shift

**Reel angle:** "OpenAI is coming to AWS and the AGI clause is gone — the two things that just quietly changed about how this whole thing works"

### 3. Sakana's Conductor: a 7B model that orchestrates frontier AI and beats them all
**What it is:** Sakana AI released Conductor — a 7B model trained with RL to coordinate a pool of frontier models (including GPT-4-class and Claude-class models). Rather than solving tasks directly, it decides which agent to call, what subtask to assign, and what context to expose. It hit 83.9% on LiveCodeBench and 87.5% on GPQA-Diamond, beating any single model in its pool.

**Why it matters:** This is the clearest demonstration yet of "AI managing AI" as an independent capability. The result — a small, cheap model outperforming the big expensive ones by routing rather than reasoning — is the kind of architectural shift worth explaining to a tech-adjacent audience. It also has a direct practical angle: you don't have to pick the best model. You can pick a cheap coordinator.

**Format:** Explainer

**Reel angle:** "A 7B model just beat GPT-4 by not doing any of the work itself — how Conductor changes what 'best AI' even means"
