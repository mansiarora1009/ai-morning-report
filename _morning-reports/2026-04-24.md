# Morning Report — 2026-04-24

## Top 3 Things Worth Paying Attention To

### 1. OpenAI turns Codex into a superapp — this is the real story behind GPT-5.5
**What it is:** GPT-5.5 launched with strong long-horizon execution, 1M context, better token efficiency, and notably lower token use per task than 5.4. But the bigger move is what OpenAI bundled with it: Codex got browser control, Sheets/Slides/Docs editing, OS-wide dictation, auto-review mode (a secondary "guardian" agent that reduces approval friction on long runs), and absorbed the defunct Prism product. OpenAI also launched Workspace Agents — Codex-powered agents with personas, external tool access (Linear, Slack), and a path to replace Custom GPTs entirely. GPT-5.5 API access is delayed pending additional safeguards.

**Why it matters:** OpenAI is executing the same playbook Anthropic ran with Claude Code, but at superapp scale. Codex is no longer a coding tool — it's a computer-use agent for QA, spreadsheets, presentations, overnight experiment runs, and research loops. The benchmark story (82.7% Terminal-Bench, 58.6% SWE-Bench Pro) matters less than the product story: the AI coding IDE race is becoming an AI work OS race. Early users describe GPT-5.5 as a step-function upgrade in autonomy — more "human," less formal — but note it can be exploratory and needs tighter instruction to stay on track.

**Format:** Shift

**Reel angle:** "GPT-5.5 is the headline — but the real move is what OpenAI did to Codex. Here's what just changed about how AI handles your computer."

### 2. DeepSeek-V4 Preview drops within hours of GPT-5.5 — MIT licensed, 1M context, aggressively priced
**What it is:** DeepSeek released DeepSeek-V4 Preview open-source under MIT: V4-Pro (1.6T total / 49B active params) and V4-Flash (284B / 13B active), both with 1M token context, thinking/non-thinking modes. Pricing: V4-Flash at $0.14/$0.28 per 1M input/output tokens; V4-Pro at $1.74/$3.48. vLLM and SGLang shipped day-0 support. The tech report highlights ~4x compute efficiency vs prior DeepSeek stacks, and order-of-magnitude KV-cache reductions. Community assessment: competitive with top closed models from the prior generation.

**Why it matters:** Artificial Analysis noted GPT-5.5 (medium) scores the same as Claude Opus 4.7 (max) at 1/4 the cost (~$1,200 vs $4,800). DeepSeek-V4-Flash takes this further: frontier-adjacent reasoning, open weights, MIT license, and V4-Flash at $0.14 input. The practical read for builders is that intelligence-per-dollar is now the real metric, not raw leaderboard position. The generation of "which frontier model is best" content is giving way to "how do I route tasks to the right cost tier."

**Format:** Shift

**Reel angle:** "The benchmark wars are over — here's the new game everyone building with AI is playing (it's about cost per task, not score)"

### 3. ChatGPT Images 2.0 finally works for knowledge workers — and most people are wasting it
**What it is:** ChatGPT Images 2.0 (also called GPT-imagegen-2) renders clean text and numbers in generated images — no garbled headers, no misread percentages, even with hundreds of words per image. Integrated with Codex so it can think, search, generate a QR code, and then compose an image. People are using it for realistic UI screenshots, multi-page illustrated magazines, and product mockups. A simple ICS prompt pattern (Image type + Content + Style) turns raw meeting notes into exec-quality slides in ~15 minutes.

**Why it matters:** Text rendering was the real blocker that kept knowledge workers away from image generation for two years — not AI skepticism. That blocker is now gone. The bottleneck shifted from "the tool can't do this" to "the user hasn't noticed yet." Ben's Bites testing found Claude Design > Magicpath AI > raw models for understanding design intent (not just pixel-copying). Ethan Mollick demonstrated GPT-5.5 + Codex creating a near-PhD-quality academic paper from 4 prompts and a 101-page illustrated TTRPG from 1 prompt. The "treat it like a party trick" crowd will be lapped this quarter.

**Format:** First Step

**Reel angle:** "The one thing that kept knowledge workers away from AI image tools just got fixed — here's what you can actually do with it now"
