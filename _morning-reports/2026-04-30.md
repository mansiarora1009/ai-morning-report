### 2. The Harness Is the Bottleneck, Not the Model
**What it is:** Multiple research threads converged this week: the "harness" around a model (memory, retrieval, tool orchestration, prompts, middleware) consistently matters more than raw model intelligence in production. A paper on Agentic Harness Engineering improved Terminal-Bench pass@1 from 69.7% to 77.0% in 10 iterations while reducing SWE-bench token use by 12% — without changing the model. HALO described self-improving agents that patch their own harness failures via trace analysis. LangChain shipped "Harness Profiles" — per-model versioned prompts, tools, and middleware.

**Why it matters:** Most people think the right move when AI disappoints is to switch models. The actual lever is almost always the scaffolding around the model: what context it has, what tools it can use, how errors are handled. This unlocks a whole reel series.

**Reel angle:** "Switching to a better model won't fix your AI workflow. This will."
