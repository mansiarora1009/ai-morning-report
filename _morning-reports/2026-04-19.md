## Top 3 Things Worth Paying Attention To

### 1. Claude Design — Anthropic enters the design tool category
**What it is:** Anthropic launched Claude Design (Anthropic Labs research preview) — natural-language prompts generate prototypes, slides, and one-pagers, with inline refinement, sliders, and exports to Canva/PPTX/PDF/HTML. Handoff to Claude Code for implementation is built in. Powered by the newly released Claude Opus 4.7.

**Why it matters:** This is Anthropic's first move beyond chat and coding. Observers immediately flagged it as a direct shot at Figma, Lovable, Bolt, and v0 — Figma's stock dropped on the announcement. AI is eating another software category, and the endgame isn't "AI helps you use Figma," it's "you describe what you want and skip Figma."

### 2. Claude Opus 4.7 killed the "extended thinking" toggle — here's what replaced it
**What it is:** Claude Opus 4.7 dropped extended thinking entirely in favor of *adaptive reasoning* — the model dynamically decides how much to think per query based on task complexity. Result: ~35% fewer output tokens at equal or better scores. Ranked #1 on Code Arena (+37 over Opus 4.6) and Text Arena. Task budgets introduced.

**Why it matters:** The shift from "you control how hard the model thinks" to "the model calibrates itself" is a meaningful UX and philosophical change. It also signals a new efficiency frontier: Opus 4.7 placed on the price/performance Pareto frontier for both text and code — ~10x fewer tokens on some ML tasks vs prior high-end models.

**Format:** Explainer

**Reel angle:** "Claude removed the thinking slider — here's what adaptive reasoning actually means and why it makes Opus 4.7 cheaper to run"

### 3. The scaffold beats the model — quietly becoming the consensus
**What it is:** A cluster of high-signal posts (surfaced in swyx's AINews) argue that reliability gains in AI systems now come more from harness design than from chasing frontier models. Example: Qwen3-8B with dspy.RLM scored 33/507 on a benchmark vs 0/507 vanilla — the scaffold did "100% of the lifting." Analysis of the leaked Claude Code harness reached the same conclusion: simple planning constraints + clean representation > fancy AI scaffolding.

**Why it matters:** The people actually shipping production AI systems have quietly moved past "which model is best." Your audience is still in that debate. This is the real unlock for going beyond basic ChatGPT use — understanding that how you structure the task matters more than the model you give it to.

**Reel angle:** "Everyone's benchmarking the wrong thing — the people shipping real AI products figured out it's the scaffold, not the model"
