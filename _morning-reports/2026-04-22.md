# Morning Report — 2026-04-22

## Top 3 Things Worth Paying Attention To

---

### 1. Claude Design is a separate product — and it's already denting Figma
**What it is:** Anthropic launched Claude Design at claude.ai/design (not inside the Claude app). Runs on Opus 4.7, which they're calling their best vision model yet. Handles wireframes, slide decks, animated videos, and high-fidelity prototypes from text prompts. Has a "Send to Canva" export. Figma lost $730M in valuation the day the news dropped.

**Why it matters:** This isn't a Canva knockoff. The power move is the DESIGN.md workflow — extract your brand system (fonts, colors, patterns) once, upload it as context, and every future generation applies it automatically. Designers who learn this can cut design time from a day to an hour. The ones who don't will keep watching AI produce generic output and declare it "not ready."

**Format:** Shift — *"Design is no longer the bottleneck. Taste is."*

**Reel angle:** "Figma lost $730M the day Anthropic launched this. Here's what it actually does."

---

### 2. GPT-Image-2 drops — and image generation just became a workflow primitive
**What it is:** OpenAI launched GPT-Image-2 across ChatGPT, Codex, and the API. It immediately hit #1 on every image Arena leaderboard — +242 Elo over the nearest competitor on text-to-image. Strong text rendering, layout fidelity, slide/infographic/UI mockup generation, and "thinking" mode for images.

**Why it matters:** The non-obvious shift: image generation is becoming the *front-end for coding agents*. The new loop is — generate a UI spec as an image, hand it to Codex or Claude Code to implement. The model isn't just for art; it's a communication layer between human intent and code agents. Already integrating with Figma, Canva, Firefly, and fal.

**Format:** Explainer — *"Why the image model race suddenly matters to developers, not just designers."*

**Reel angle:** "You're not generating art. You're briefing your code agent. Here's the new workflow."

---

### 3. Open-weight models are closing the gap — and the moat is moving to the harness
**What it is:** Moonshot's Kimi K2.6 (1T parameter MoE, open-source) launched this week and Reddit is on fire. Top posts: "Kimi K2.6 is a legit Opus 4.7 replacement" (1,600+ upvotes), "Opus 4.7 Max subscriber. Switching to Kimi 2.6." Simultaneously, the AINews consensus is that "the useful part of agent systems is increasingly the runtime/harness, not the base model."

**Why it matters:** The playbook is shifting. Power users aren't asking "which frontier model?" — they're asking "which harness?" Claude Code, Kimi 2.6 Code, DSPy 3.2, LangChain deepagents. The model underneath is becoming interchangeable for many tasks. The lock-in is now the workflow, memory, and tooling you build around it.

**Format:** Shift — *"The model isn't the moat anymore."*

**Reel angle:** "People are leaving Claude Pro for an open-source model. Here's what that tells you about where AI is actually going."
