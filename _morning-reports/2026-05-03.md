## Top 3 Things Worth Paying Attention To

### 1. How to make Claude write in your voice — the "voice file" method
**What it is:** A two-step Claude workflow for capturing your writing voice in a portable .md file: first, a 100-question "Taste Interviewer" prompt that interviews you about your beliefs, sentence shapes, aesthetic crimes, and hard refusals; then a "Voice Compiler" prompt that compresses the 20k-word transcript into a 2–4k token `about-me.md`. Paste the file into any Claude/ChatGPT session and it writes first drafts that sound like you. Ruben Hassid (520k readers) published the exact prompts and described using Claude Opus 4.7 + Extended Thinking + Wispr Flow for voice dictation.

**Why it matters:** Most people prompting "write in my style" get a pale imitation. This is systematic: it forces specificity (Claude pushes back on vague answers), compresses to what *actually changes output*, and makes your voice portable across models. It's a workflow shift from "use AI to write" to "encode yourself into AI."

**Reel angle:** "How to make Claude actually write like you — not like ChatGPT trying to guess your style"

### 2. DeepSeek V4 and the collapsing cost of frontier AI
**What it is:** DeepSeek dropped V4-Pro (1.6T params / 49B active, 1M context, MIT license) at $1.74/M input — the cheapest large frontier model, undercutting Claude Opus 4.7 and Gemini 3.1 Pro by a wide margin. V4-Flash ($0.14/M input) is the cheapest small model and may run quantized on an M5 MacBook. On the Intelligence Index, leading open-weight models (DeepSeek V4-Pro, Kimi K2.6, MiMo V2.5) now score 52–54 vs closed frontier at 57–60. DeepSeek's self-assessment: "trailing state-of-the-art by approximately 3 to 6 months."

**Why it matters:** The open/closed gap is now a rounding error. For Mansi's audience, the practical implication is that the "best model" question is getting stale — a DeepSeek V4-Pro agent at $1.74/M vs Claude Opus 4.7 at ~$15/M shifts the economics of AI workflows dramatically. The bottleneck is no longer the model; it's knowing what to do with it.

**Format:** Shift

**Reel angle:** "The model gap is closing. The people winning at AI aren't using the most expensive model — here's what they're doing instead"

### 3. Codex vs Claude Code: it's a harness race, not a model race
**What it is:** OpenAI is shipping Codex features at high velocity: 30% faster browser-use, CI status in chat, device toolbar for responsive testing, and an agent import/migration system. The framing from practitioners (per AINews / swyx) has shifted away from "which model is smarter" to "which environment feels right." @theo's current take: GPT-5.5 is faster, more direct, unblocks you faster; Claude Opus 4.7 has better taste and intent but slower TTFT and more tool calls. Cursor simultaneously launched a TypeScript SDK for building programmatic agents — "like Claude Code but programmable."

**Why it matters:** The competitive surface has moved from raw model IQ to harness design: subagents, durable state, browser-use, context compaction, and feedback loops. Your audience is still picking tools by benchmark — the practitioners have already moved past that to asking "which environment fits my workflow."

**Format:** Explainer

**Reel angle:** "GPT-5.5 vs Claude Code: the honest take from people who use both every day"
