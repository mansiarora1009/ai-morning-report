### 2. The internet is being rewired for agents, not humans
**What it is:** Cloudflare now lets AI agents autonomously provision accounts, register domains, and deploy — humans just approve terms. Stripe's Sessions announced an Agentic Commerce Suite with agent-native wallets that issue one-time payment credentials, streaming payments, and agent-ready Treasury accounts. The pattern: critical internet infrastructure is adding agent-facing APIs as first-class citizens.

**Why it matters:** When agents can buy domains and pay for services on your behalf, "agentic workflow" stops meaning "AI that drafts emails" and starts meaning "AI that runs business operations." This is the plumbing shift that makes autonomous agents actually autonomous.

**Reel angle:** "Stripe and Cloudflare just gave AI agents a credit card and a server — what happens next?"

### 3. Harness engineering is now the real competitive edge — Cursor published their playbook
**What it is:** Cursor released a detailed breakdown of how they test and tune their agent harness: bespoke prompts per model, mixed offline/online evals, degradation repair, treating the context window as the primary compute boundary. The Cursor SDK also dropped — letting anyone build agents on Cursor's own runtime, harness, and models. The discourse around this crystallized: the winners aren't picking the best model, they're engineering the best wrapper.

**Why it matters:** This is the first time a leading AI coding tool has published this level of detail on harness engineering. It's a signal that the craft of "how you wrap the model" is where the real differentiation lives — and it's teachable.
