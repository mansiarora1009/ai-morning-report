### 2. "You're just a text file" — Ruben's method for making any AI write exactly like you
**What it is:** Ruben (How to AI, 520k subscribers) published a full playbook: run a 100-question voice interview with Claude (using a detailed "Taste Interviewer" prompt), then compress the 20k-word output into a 2,000–4,000 token `about-me.md` file using a "Voice Compiler" prompt. Upload this file to Claude Cowork so every session starts with your voice as context. Also works with ChatGPT, Gemini, or any AI. The compression step is the key unlock — most people stop at the raw dump, which is too token-heavy to be practical.

**Why it matters:** This is the cleanest, most actionable "write like me" workflow anyone has published. The insight isn't "AI can mimic you" — it's that **you become portable**: hand the file to a ghostwriter, your team's drafts, customer service, or any AI that ships next. The "maximum behavioral fidelity per token" framing is also a useful lens for anyone building AI workflows.

For your audience — people who want to go beyond basic AI use — this is the most important conceptual shift right now: the leverage point has moved from "pick the best model" to "structure the task correctly."
